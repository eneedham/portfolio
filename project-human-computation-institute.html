Working to Prevent Female Genital Mutilation: Analyzing Access to Healthcare and Education in Tanzania

Female genital mutilation (FGM) persists as a major risk to the health and wellbeing of young girls and women in certain parts of East Africa. Working in partnership with the Tanzania Development Trust and The Human Computation Institute we analyzed factors that may be influential to the risk of FGM. This included calculating access to healthcare services and education. We found that access to healthcare and membership in certain clans were the highest correlated factors. For more information, check out the Summer of Maps project description.
