My name is Esther. I'm an environmentalist, urbanist, and creator of maps and data visualizations. I work as a project manager, geospatial analyst, and freelance planner. Many of my projects are related to the environment, sustainability, and equity, such as multi-modal transit, factors influencing community health, and the impacts of redlining. I love what I do and am inspired by the challenge of turning numbers into a visual representation of our world.

I often give presentations about my work and host GIS workshops and mapathons. I'm a supporter and contributor to OpenStreetMap.

Experience & Skills

I graduated from the University of Pennsylvania with a master's degree in City and Regional Planning. I concentrated in land use and environmental planning and earned a certificate in GIS and Spatial Analysis. Since then I have been working at Azavea, a geospatial technolgoy B-Corporation based out of Philadelphia, PA. In addition, I take on freelance planning and geospatial work and work on personal projects. While a student, I interned for the Environment Division of Antigua and Barbuda and for the Philadelphia City Planning Commission.

These days, the majority of my work is completed using open source geospatial and design tools, though I'm well-versed in the Esri environment and Adobe Suite. I build reproducible data pipelines using R, Python, PostGIS, and gdal and create graphics with ggplot2, QGIS, D3, and Figma.
